Fatal Car Crashes By Type in 2019

In 2019 there were 36,096 people killed in traffic crashes. The rate at which drivers experience fatal crashes varies across the states (and District of Columbia). Many factors can affect these rates, including types of vehicles driven, travel speeds, rates of licensure, state traffic laws, emergency care capabilities, weather and topography. The map and chart below displays the total fatal car crash rate per 100 million vehicle miles traveled (VMT) in each state. Additionally, the fatal car crashes can be viewed by the following types: motor vehicle collision, nonmotorist collision, fixed object collision, object not fixed collsion, and non-collision overturn.

The data shown in this map was obtained from the NHTSA Fatality Analysis Reporting System (FARS) & USDOT FHA Highway Statistics Series.
