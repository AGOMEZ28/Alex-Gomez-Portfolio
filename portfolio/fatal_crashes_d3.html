Fatal Car Crashes By Type in 2019

Every year thousands of drivers in the U.S. are involved in fatal car crashes. The rate at which drivers experience fatal crashes varies across the states (and Disrict of Columbia). Many factors can affect these rates, including types of vehicles driven, travel speeds, rates of licensure, state traffic laws, emergency care capabilities, weather and topography. The map and chart below displays the fatal car crash rate per 100 million vehicle miles traveled in each state. Additionally, the fatal car crashes can be viewed by type, such as: motorist collision, nonmotorist collision, object fixed type, object not fixed type, and non collision. The data shown in this map was obtained from the NHTSA Fatality Analysis Reporting System (FARS) & USDOT FHA Highway Statistics Series.
